Ceviche

"En el Peru no existen ceviches malos, no hay Ceviches malos. Todos los ceviches en este pais son buenos"

Peru's national dish and it's most recogizable, Ceviche. Ceviche is a seafood dish made from fresh fish cured in fresh citrus juices, most commonly lemon or lime.

The other ingredients that are added include ají, chopped onions, salt, and pepper.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds very fresh and high quality fish filets (corvina, tilapia, halibut, escolar, or hamachi
  • 1 red onion thinly sliced
  • 1 cup freshly squeezed lime juice from about 35-40 key limes, or 15-20 Peruvian limes
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 3 Cloves of crushed garlic
  • 2 Table spoons of Aji amarillo paste

Recipe Instructions

  1. Cut the fish into small cubes.
  2. In a large bowl squeeze out the juice from all your limes.
  3. If you have peruvian limes add one tablespoon of salt, if using key limes add 2 ½ tablespoons of salt.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of ground pepper, then mix and taste test.
  5. Add your fish and onions into the bowl with the lime juice.
  6. Then add your crush garlic and aji paste.
  7. Place in the fridge for 25 minutes.
  8. Take it out and serve (sweet potato and peruvian corn are optional).
