Tallarines Verdes

"Hijito lindo hoy cocine tu favorito, ven sientate a comer"

Tallarines Verdes is my favorite dish of all time, my grandmother used to cook it for me all the time when I was younger.

The dish itself is a variaton of pesto and was initially created when Italian immigrants that settled in Peru brought over the original recipe.

This peruvian variant is creamier as it uses cheese local to latin countries as well as evaporated milk while cutting out the pine nuts from the original recipe.

Ingredients

  • 200 grams of spinach
  • 200 grams fresh basil (Same amount as spinach if you change measurements)
  • 350 grams queso fresco
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • Salt
  • 3/4 quarters of vegetable or olive oil
  • 1 can of evaporated milk
  • 1 package of your preferred pasta

Recipe Instructions

  1. Cut your cheese into cubes
  2. Take out your blender then put in a quarter of the spinach, basil, and cheese into blender.
  3. Into the blender add half of the evaporated milk, oil, and garlic.
  4. Blend on high until a cohesive sauce is made.
  5. Stop the blender then again add a quarter of the spinach, basil, and cheese into blender.
  6. Add the last of the evaporated milk, oil and garlic.
  7. Blend on high until smooth
  8. Stop blender then add the last of your spinach, basil, and cheese into blender.
  9. Blend again until sauce is creamy then add salt to taste.
  10. Make your desired choice of pasta how you like, as you're boiling your pasta reserve a 1/4 cup of the pasta water
  11. Drain your pasta, put it back in the pot, add your sauce over the pasta then add your pasta water. Mix well then serve.
